Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
The sheet has few indications as to which strings are used and there is no
reference video - feel free to change the left hand fingering in the tab.
The MIDI is just a guide to the notes. It is much slower than the Eva Beneke
recording, and lacks the necessary changes in tempo to provide a pleasing
interpretation of the piece.
The starting tempo of the MIDI is 56 bpm (quarter notes); the tempo at the
start of the Presto section is 96 bpm (quarter notes).

measure 7  - the sheet indicates sliding the fingers up from the notes of the
             1st interval to the notes of the 2nd interval, but in the audio
             the notes themselves are slide up, with the notes of the 2nd
             interval not being plucked - the notes of the 3rd interval, when
             the fingers slide back, are played normally - this happens every
             time a similar pattern of 3 intervals is played
measure 9  - in the sheet the 4th note is F, but Eva Beneke plays F# - the 4th
             note in the equivalent measure 68 is F#
measure 13 - in the sheet the time signature is 4/4, but the last 4 notes are
             1/32nd notes, which leaves the measure incomplete by an 1/8th note
             - in the equivalent measure 83 the last 4 notes are 1/16th notes
             followed by 6 1/32nd grace notes
measure 26 - in the sheet the 7th note is E, but Eva Beneke plays D# - the 7th
             note in the equivalent measure 33 is D#
measure 78 - Eva Beneke arpeggiates (ripples) the chord and plays the F# on
             the 2nd string before the E on the open 1st string
