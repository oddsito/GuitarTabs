Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
The MIDI is at the given tempo of 76 bpm (beats per minute - quarter notes).
The tempo of the Max Coradduzza reference video is much slower, ~54 bpm.

left hand fingering (LHF) below tab     right hand fingering (RHF) above tab
1 - index finger (forefinger)           p - thumb
2 - middle finger                       i - index finger (forefinger)
3 - ring finger                         m - middle finger
4 - little finger (pinky)               a - ring finger

mordents (M) -    measure 6          measure 10          measure 17
                 |---------         |----------         |-2h3p2----
                 |-2h3p2---         |----------         |----------
                 |---------         |-0h2p0----         |----------
                 |---------         |----------         |----------
                 |---------         |----------         |----------
                 |---------         |----------         |----------
                   1 2 1                2                 1 2 1

===    - note held, length of note, duration
=-=    - let the note ring, don't dampen the sound
'      - slight pause (before the next note), breath mark (luftpause)
/      - slide the finger up to the next note
\      - slide the finger down to the next note
|-3-|  - triplet - 3 notes played in the time of 2 notes
< < <  - (below the tab) crescendo - growing louder
> > >  - (below the tab) decrescendo - becoming softer
arp.   - arpeggiate - rupple upwards from the lowest note to the highest notes
c2     - part barre on the 2nd fret
dolce  - sweetly
h      - hammer-on
F.     - Fermata - notes held for longer than their value
g      - grace note - ornament - an extra note played on or just before the
         main note and immediately released - the following note is usually
         played as a hammer-on, a pull-off or sometimes a slide
l.v.   - laissez vibrer (let vibrate) - let it ring, don't dampen the sound
M      - ornament - upper mordent - play the main (melody) note, followed by
         the note (in the scale) above, and then return to the original note
p      - (between the notes) pull-off
rall.  - rallentando - slowing down (often becoming gradually softer)
rit.   - ritenuto - suddenly slower
sempre - always, continue like this throughout the piece
