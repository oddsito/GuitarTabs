Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
Originally published by Gio. Ricordi, September 1835. Thematic index:
1) Dall'aura tua profetica - Coro from Norma, Act 1, Scene 1
2) A fosco cielo, a notte buia - Coro from La Sonnambula, Act 1, Scene 6
3) Un ultimo addio - Duetta from La Straniera, Act 1, Scene 7 Alaide e Arturo
4) Si, fino all'ore estreme - Duetto from Norma, Act 2 Scene 2, Norma e Adalgisa

Part measures at the start of sections are numbered to help with referencing
(measures 35, 91, 149, 185). The DGA edition gives very little information
about the fingering so feel free to experiment in order to improve the phrasing.
The MIDI and the Paolo Amico reference audio do not play the repeats.

measure 10 - in the sheet the 5th note is G (not G#)
measure 51 - in the sheet there are only 3 beats (the rest on beat 4 is missing)
measure 104 - in the sheet the Bs on the 2nd and 3rd strings are B#s (?)
measure 283 - in the sheet the bottom note of the 1st chord (open D, 4th string)
              is a quarter note (not an eighth note), and the 2nd note (D on the
              12 the fret) is a 1/16th note (not an 1/8th note)

left hand fingering below the tab -
1 - index finger (forefinger)
2 - middle finger
3 - ring finger
4 - little finger (pinkie)

===    - note held, length of note, duration
|-3-|  - triplet - 3 notes played in the time of 2 notes
|-6-|  - hextuplet - 6 notes played in the time of 4 notes
C1     - barre on the 1st fret
C2     - barre on the 2nd fret
F.     - Fermata - pause (either rest, or notes held)
g      - grace note(s) - ornament - extra note(s) played just before the main
         note, usually as a pull-off or hammer-on (or sometimes a slide)
h      - hammer-on
larg.  - largando - growing broader, slower and more marked with a crescendo
p      - (below the tab) piano - soft
p      - (between the notes) pull-off
poco   - little, a little (poco a poco - little by little)
rall.  - rallentando - slowing down (sometimes becoming gradually softer)
sf     - sforzando - forced, strongly accented, suddenly loud
tempo  - speed (a tempo - return to the earlier tempo)
