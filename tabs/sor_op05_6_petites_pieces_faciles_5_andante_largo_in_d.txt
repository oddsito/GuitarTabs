Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
Although the title means 'Six little very easy pieces', some of the stretches
are difficult to play on a modern classical guitar.

The Simrock and the Stich und Druck editions show more repeated sections, but
some of the repeat signs are missing (which is confusing). The tab follows the
three reference videos, which only repeat the first section in the sheet.

The MIDI tempo is 56 bpm (beats per minute, eighth notes).
The order of play is measures 0-8, 1-7, 9-45, 0-7, 9-25.

measure 5 - in the Stich und Druck edition the 4th note is D (not D#)
measure 7-9 - Hiromu Taguchi plays the last chord of measure 7 and the first
              chord of measures 8 & 9 with a barre on the 2nd fret
            - the Lopes edition has A-C#-E-A for the 1st chord of measures 8
              and 9 (not A-A-C#-A)
measure 11 - in the Lopes edition the 1st chord is played with a barre
measure 16 - in the Simrock and Stich und Druck editions the 13th note is G#
measure 22 - the Lopes edition doesn't have an open G in the 2nd chord
measures 22,29 - (similar to m18) the bass notes should probably be staccato
measure 23 - in the Simrock edition the chord on the 2nd beat is G#-D-F#
measure 25 - in the Simrock and Stich und Druck editions the G & E on the 1st
             beat are both played as an appoggiatura (ornament - passing note)
             and the E on the open 6th string is played with the F# & D - the
             tab follows the 3 reference videos which play the E with the G & E
measure 27 - in the Simrock edition the 3rd interval is E#-G# (not E-G#)
measure 34 - in the Lopes edition the chord on the 2nd beat is D-D-F (not D-E-F)
measure 39 - Hiromu Taguchi sems to play the trill as an upper mordent (?) -
             Tatyana Ryzhkova and Edson Lopes both ignore the trill
measure 41 - Tatyana Ryzhkova plays G as the bottom note on the 1st beat (not D)
measure 44 - in the Simrock edition the last chord is A-E-G#-C# (not A-E-G-C#)

===    - note held, length of note, duration
'      - (above the tab) slight pause, breath mark (luftpause)
|-6-|  - sextuplet - 6 notes played in the time of 4 notes
D.C.   - Da Capo - return to the start
Fine   - end, the end of the piece (al Fine - continue until Fine)
g      - grace note (ornament) - acciaccatura - an extra note played as quickly
         as possible before the main note, and immediately released - usually
         played as a hammer-on or pull-off or slide
h      - hammer-on
p      - pull-off
s      - staccato - detached, cut off, emphatic, note not held for full value
tr.    - trill (ornament) - a rapid alternation of a note with the note above it
         in the scale (usually starting on the note above)

c1     - part barre on the 1st fret
C1     - barre on the 1st fret
c2     - part barre on the 2nd fret
C2     - barre on the 2nd fret [etc]

repeat the section    1____  play the 1st time        2_______  play on repeat
