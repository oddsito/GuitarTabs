Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

left hand fingering (LHF)          right hand fingering (RHF)
1 - index finger                   p - thumb
2 - middle finger                  i - index finger (forefinger)
3 - ring finger                    m - middle finger
4 - little finger                  a - ring finger (annular)

CII    barre on 2nd fret
CIV    barre on 4rd fret
CV     barre on 5th fret
CVII   barre on 7th fret

mezzo forte    mf - medium loud
forte          f  - loud
piano          p  - piano
mezzo-piano    mp - medium soft

express.   - expressively
arp.       - arpeggiate - ripple upwards from lowest note to highest note
cresc.     - crescendo - becoming gradually louder
decresc.   - decrescendo - becoming gradually softer
w          - upper mordent (ornament) - play the main (melody) note, followed by
             the note above (in the scale), and then return to the original note
menos      - less (volume), quieter, (also slower?)
Lejano     - lontano - distant, remote, as from far away
reten.     - retenido(?) - restrained, held back
lentamente - slowly (p y lentamente - softly and slowly)

h    - hammer-on
p    - pull-off
>    - accented, marked, stressed
F.   - Fermata - pause, notes held for longer than their value
g    - grace note (ornament) - extra notes played immediately
       before the main notes

<5>    - natural harmonic on the 5th fret
<12>   - natural harmonic on the 12th fret
2[14]  - artificial harmonic, play 2nd fret, point to the 14th fret
3[15]  - artificial harmonic, play 3rd fret, point to the 15th fret
4[16]  - artificial harmonic, play 4th fret, point to the 16th fret
5[17]  - artificial harmonic, play 5th fret, point to the 17th fret
7[19]  - artificial harmonic, play 7th fret, point to the 19th fret

|-3-| - triplet - 3 notes played in the time of 2 notes
