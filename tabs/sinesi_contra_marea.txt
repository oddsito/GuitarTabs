Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
Order of play: 0-17, 1-13, 18-42, 0-13, 43-61, 58-64

measure 51 - the first 4 notes on the 2nd beat (2nd string) are 32nd notes

left hand fingering below the tab -
1 - index finger
2 - middle finger
3 - ring finger
4 - little finger

===     - note held, length of note, duration
/       - (below the tab) slide the finger up to play the next note
\       - (below the tab) slide the finger down to play the next note
< < <   - crescendo - becoming gradually louder
> > >   - decrescendo - becoming gradually softer
boca    - pluck the notes with the right hand over the soundhole
bpm     - beats per minute (quarter notes)
cresc.  - becoming gradually louder
Coda    - the end section of the piece
D.C.    - Da Capo - return to the beginning of the piece
f       - forte - loud
ff      - fortissimo - very loud
F.      - Fermata - notes held for longer than their duration, pause
g       - grace note, ornament - extra note played before the main note -
          the next note is usually played as a hammer-on, pull-off or slide
h       - hammer-on
mf      - mezzoforte - moderately loud
p       - (below the tab) piano - soft
p       - (between the notes) pull-off
pont.   - ponticello - pluck the notes with the right hand close to the bridge
rall.   - rallentando - slowing down
s       - staccato - detached, cut off, the note is not held for its full value
sul     - by, at, near, above (sul tasto - over the fingerboard)
tasto   - pluck the notes with the right hand over the fingerboard
<7>     - natural (open) harmonic on the 7th fret
<12>    - natural (open) (octave) harmonic on the 12th fret
<19>    - natural (open) harmonic on the 19th fret

c1      - part barre on the 1st fret
C1      - barre on the 1st fret
c2      - part barre on the 2nd fret
C2      - barre on the 2nd fret
etc
