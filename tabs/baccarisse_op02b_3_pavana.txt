Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
"Salvador Bacarisse's Pavana is a little known work written for guitar around
1930. The work was originally the third movement of Bacarisse's piano piece,
Heraldos (1923), and was arranged by the composer at the request of the
guitarist, Regino Sainz de la Maza." (Anthony LaLena)
The Yiannis Efstathopoulos audio recording is one semitone lower starting in
the key of Ab minor.

Pavana     - slow processional dance common in Europe during the 16th century
arp        - arpeggiate
cresc      - crescendo - becoming gradually louder
rit        - ritenuto - slowing down, suddenly slower
Poco meno  - a little slower
Rubato     - expressive and rhythmic freedom (temporary disregard strict tempo)
a tempo    - at normal speed
animando   - animated, lively
pesante    - in a heavy manner, ponderous
poco piu   - a little faster
Ossia      - alternative passage which may be played instead of the original
vivo       - in a lively, brisk manner
molto      - much, very
Tempo I    - return to the original tempo
F.         - Fermata - pause, notes held
perdendosi - gradually diminishing in volume
>          - accented, stressed

|-3-| - triplet    - 3 notes played in the time of 2 notes
|-6-| - sextuplet  - 6 notes played in the time of 4 notes

<9>    - natural harmonic on the 9th fret
<12>   - natural harmonic on the 12th fret
<19>   - natural harmonic on the 19th fret
1[13]  - artificial harmonic, play 1st fret, point to the 13th fret
2[14]  - artificial harmonic, play 2nd fret, point to the 14th fret
3[15]  - artificial harmonic, play 3rd fret, point to the 15th fret
4[16]  - artificial harmonic, play 4th fret, point to the 16th fret
5[17]  - artificial harmonic, play 5th fret, point to the 17th fret
6[18]  - artificial harmonic, play 6th fret, point to the 18th fret
