Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
"Segovia was selective and only performed works with which he identified
personally. He was known to reject atonal works, or works which he considered
too radical, even if they were dedicated to him; e.g. he rejected Frank Martin's
Quatre pieces breves, Darius Milhaud's Segoviana etc." (Wikipedia)

There is no key signature, but the opening and closing sounds like G major.
The difference isn't always clear between the markings for hammer-ons/pull-offs
and phrase marks, so they have been left out of the tab. Use them where they
seem appropriate (eg for the last beat of measure 10).
The starting tempo of the MIDI is 72 bpm (quarter notes), which is slower than
the sheet tempo, but faster than most of the reference videos.
The sheet does not give any left hand fingering, and the runs are played in
different ways by different guitarists (higher up or lower down the neck) -

- https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4IeAYoWQn1o - Andrea Gonzalez Caballero
- https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WIsGJosOXQA - Brendon Grabowski
- https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8FMpqgBobQ - Francesco Palmieri
- https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GLS2ew5OL2g - Francesco Taliento
- https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S49GULHqBys - Gonzalo Antuna
- https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vn5YgEPSlJQ - Sanel Redzic

measure 11 - in the sheet the last note is a 1/4 note (not a dotted 1/4 note)
measures 47,48,57 - in the sheet the 3 note chord has the middle D doubled (?)
                    (played on 2 strings) which is a difficult if not impossible
                    stretch either downwards to the 3rd fret or upwards to the
                    12th fret - the tab follows the reference videos
measure 56 - this is in 3/8 time (twice the speed of measure 55 in 3/4 time)
