Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
Jose Antonio Carlos de Seixas was one of Portugal's most prolific composers.
He is thought to have written more than 700 works, most of which were lost
during an earthquake that destroyed much of Lisbon in 1755.

In the reference video, Rebeca Oliveira uses a capo on the 2nd fret.
The MIDI tempo of the Adagio is 30 bpm (quarter notes), of the Jiga 120 bpm,
and of the Minuet 116 bpm.

measure 6 - the last 2 notes are a part barre held with the 4th finger

measures 39,41,47 - in the sheet the grace notes are written as acciacaturas,
                    ("squeezed in" notes) but in the Rebeca Oliveira reference
                    video they are played as appoggiaturas (passing notes)

Left Hand Fingering (LHF) below the tab -
1 - index finger (forefinger)
2 - middle finger
3 - ring finger
4 - little finger (pinkie)

===    - note held
'      - (measure 2) wedge - slightly accented, semi-staccato (separated)
|-3-|  - triplet - 3 notes in the time of 2 notes
bpm    - beats per minute (quarter notes)
C1     - barre on the 1st fret
C2     - barre on the 2nd fret
C3     - barre on the 3rd fret
c6     - (measure 7) part barre on the 6th fret with the 4th finger
g      - grace note - ornament - an extra note played at the same time as,
         or just before the main note, then released
h      - hammer-on
p      - (between the notes) pull-off

c1     - part barre on the 1st fret
C1     - barre on the 1st fret
c2     - part barre on the 2nd fret
C2     - barre on the 2nd fret [etc]

Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
Jose Antonio Carlos de Seixas was one of Portugal's most prolific composers.
He is thought to have written more than 700 works, most of which were lost
during an earthquake that destroyed much of Lisbon in 1755.

In the reference video, Rebeca Oliveira uses a capo on the 2nd fret.
In performance, for repeated sections it is common for a section first to be
played without ornaments, and on the repeat to be played with ornaments.
In the reference video, the tempo of the Adagio is 30 bpm (quarter notes),
of the Jiga 120 bpm and of the Minuet 116 bpm.

The measures with ossia/ornaments are 3-9,17,19,28,32,34,38,39,43,45,47.

measure 6 - the last 2 notes are a part barre held with the 4th finger

measure 3 - in the sheet the grace note is written as an acciacatura,
            ("squeezed in" note) but in the Rebeca Oliveira reference
            video it is played as an appoggiatura (passing note)

|-3-|  - triplet - 3 notes in the time of 2 notes

g      - grace note - ornament - extra note played at the same time as,
         or just before the main note, then released
h      - hammer-on
M      - lower mordent (ornament) - play the main note, quickly followed by
         the note below (in the scale), then quickly back to the original note
p      - (between the notes) pull-off
tr     - trill (ornament) - a rapid alternation of a note with the note above
         it in the scale, usually starting on the note above
