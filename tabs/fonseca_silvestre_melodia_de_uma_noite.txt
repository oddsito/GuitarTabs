Notes & Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
Duo version: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HvryUSrUUhI - Edson Lopes (2023)
The given tempo is 130 bpm (beats per minute - quarter notes) but this is only
a guide to the faster passages - much of the piece is played much slower. (The
initial MIDI tempo is 116 bpm, and it is ~30sec slower than the reference vids.)
The slides to the grace notes land on the note to be played next.
Where 2 or more notes are played together, the RHF refers to the upper note.
The order of play is 1-34, 1-34, 35-45.

measure 18 - in the sheet the upper note of the 1st interval (B 1st string 7th
             fret) is held though out the measure and for a beat and a half
             of the following measure, but the fingering for the other notes
             in the measure (and also in the reference vids) is like the tab
measure 33 - in the sheet the upper note of the 1st interval (A 4th string 7th
             fret) is held thoughout the measure (not the 2nd A)

left hand fingering (LHF) below tab      right hand fingering (RHF) above tab
1 - index finger (forefinger)            p - thumb
2 - middle finger                        i - index finger (forefinger)
3 - ring finger                          m - middle finger
4 - little finger (pinky)                a - ring finger

===    - notes held, length of note, duration
/      - (between notes) slide the note up - (below the tab) slide the finger up
\      - (below tab) slide the note down - (below the tab) slide the finger down
< < <  - crescendo - becoming gradually louder
> > >  - decrescendo - becoming gradually softer
|-5-|  - pentuplet - 5 notes played in the time of 4 notes
<12>   - natural (open) octave harmonic on the 12th fret
arp.   - arpeggiate - ripple upwards from the lowest note to the highest note
bpm    - beats per minute (quarter notes)
cresc. - crescendo - becoming gradually louder
f      - forte - loud, strong
F.     - Fermata - notes held for longer than their value
g      - grace note (ornament) - an extra note played before the main note -
         the next note is usually played as a hammer-on, pull-off or slide
mf     - mezzo-forte - moderately loud
p      - (below the tab) piano - quiet, softly
p      - (between the notes) pull-off
pizz.  - pizzicato - muted (usually played by plucking the string with the
         right thumb and using the palm of the hand to mute the sting)
rall.  - rallentando - slowing down, broadening out, sometimes softening
rit.   - ritenuto - slowing down, becoming suddenly slower
t      - tenuto - played firmly, note(s) held for at least the full length
tempo  - speed (a tempo - return to the earlier tempo after rit.)

c1     - part barre on the 1st fret
C1     - barre on the 1st fret
c2     - part barre on the 2nd fret
C2     - barre on the 2nd fret [etc]
