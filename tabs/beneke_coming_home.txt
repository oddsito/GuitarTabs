Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
The tempo of the MIDI is 88 bpm (beats per minute - quarter notes).
The order of play is measures 1-25, 18-24, 26, 4-24, 26, 1-24, 26-34. (?)
The order of play in the Eva Beneke reference video is 1-24, 26, 4-24, 26-34.

measure 1-3 - these are played rubato (not in a strict tempo, but in the
              performer's own time)

left hand fingering below tab       right hand fingering above tab
1 - index finger (forefinger)       p - thumb
2 - middle finger                   i - index finger (forefinger)
3 - ring finger                     m - middle finger
4 - little finger (pinky)           a - ring finger

===    - note held (duration)
=-=    - let the notes continue to ring, don't dampen the sound
>      - accented, marked, stresssed
|-5-|  - 5 notes played in the time of 4 notes 
$      - (start of measure 4) segno - sign
D.S.   - Dal Segno - return to the sign (start of measure 4)
f      - forte - loud
F.     - Fermata - note held, pause
Fine   - (measure 26) end (of the piece) (al Fine - and continue to the end)
g      - grace note, ornament - extra note played before the main note -
         the next note is usually played as a hammer-on, pull-off or slide
h      - hammer-on
mf     - mezzoforte - moderately loud
pp     - pianissimo - very soft
rit.   - slowing down, becoming suddenly slower

repeat the section    1____  play the 1st time       2_______  play on repeat
