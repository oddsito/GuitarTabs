Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
Both the Leonhard Schulz edition and the JG Holm MS transcription are missing
some of the accidentals, especially flat signs for the Bs.

The tempo of the MIDI is 94 bpm (beats per minute - quarter notes).

measure 12 - the sheet fingering isn't clear (there is no barre on the 9th fret)
measure 12 - in the JG Holm transcription the crescendo is for the whole measure 
measure 13 - the Otto Tolonen video follows the JG Holm transcription, playing
             an octave interval (A-A) on the 1st beat -

                |
             e|/17--
             B|-----
             G|-14--
             D|-----
             A|-----
             E|-----

left hand fingering (LHF) below the tab -
1 - index finger (forefinger)
2 - middle finger
3 - ring finger (annular)
4 - little finger (pinky)

===      - note held, length of note, duration
/        - slide the note up
< < <    - (below the tab) crescendo - growing louder
> > >    - (below the tab) decrescendo - growing softer
Agitato  - agitated, with emotion, excited, fast, hurried, restless, anxious
f        - forte - loud, strong
ff       - fortissimmo - very loud
g        - grace note, ornament - an extra note played immediately before the
           main note - at the start of measure 9 it is played as a slide up to
           the 11th fret - the hammer-on and pull-off on the 3rd beat of the
           measure are also shown as grace notes in the sheet
h        - hammer-on
p        - (beneath the tab) piano - soft, quiet
p        - (between the notes) pull-off
risoluto - bold, resolute, energetic
ritard.  - ritardando - slowing down

c1       - part barre on the 1st fret
C1       - barre on the 1st fret
c2       - part barre on the 2nd fret
C2       - barre on the 2nd fret [etc]
