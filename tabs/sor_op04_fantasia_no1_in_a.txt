Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
The piece was first printed by the London music publisher L Larenuin in
about 1815-18 without an opus number, and was called "A 2nd Fantasia".
In later Parisian editions it was given the opus number 4 and called
'Deuxieme Fantaisie'.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
"In the original edition of this Rondo, the sequence of the repeats and of the
recurring sections appears to be corrupt. It has been conjectually restored
here. The repeats [starting] at bars 99 and 159 [are] probably optional.
Bar 84: the repeat sign is editorial.
Bar 125, rest: in the original it is a turn: in fact it could be a turn, but
it may well be a misprint for a rest.
Bar 137: the notation is unorthodox but clear enough.
The Meissonnier edition... has been compared." - Brian Jeffery
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
