Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

left hand fingering (LHF) above and below tab      right hand fingering (RHF)
1 - index finger                                   p - thumb
2 - middle finger                                  i - index finger (forefinger)
3 - ring finger                                    m - middle finger
4 - little finger                                  a - ring finger (annular)
                                                   e - little finger
CI      barre on 1st fret
CII     barre on 2nd fret
CIII    barre on 3rd fret
CIV     barre on 4th fret
CVIII   barre on 7th fret
CIX     barre on 9th fret

mp  - mezzo-piano - medium soft
f   - forte - loud
pp  - pianissimo - very soft
p   - piano - soft
ff  - fortissimo - very loud (a ff - up to ff)
mf  - mezzo-forte - medium loud

Andantino          - slightly faster than andante
festoso            - festive, lively, joyful
in rilievo         - in relief, slightly louder
delicatamente      - delicately
|-6-|              - hextuplet - 6 notes in the time of 4 notes
s                  - staccato - detached, cut off, note not held for full value
poco a poco        - little by little, gradually
cresc              - crescendo - becoming gradually louder
decresc            - decrescendo - becoming gradually softer
'                  - slight pause, breathmark
Andante tranquillo - moderately slow, quietly
scuro              - dark
dolce              - sweetly, gently
l.v.               - laissez vibrer (let vibrate) - let the notes ring
dim                - diminuendo - diminishing, becoming softer
F                  - Fermata - pause (lunga F - a long pause)
Precipitando       - in a hurried or headlong manner
pizz Bartok        - Bartok pizzicato - pull the string from the fingerboard
                     so that it snaps back percussively
