Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
The tempo of the MIDI is 42 bpm (beats per minute - dotted quarter notes).

measure 35 - in the sheet the B and A in the 1st chord are quarter notes,
             which makes the following pull-off on the 1st string implausible

left hand fingering (LHF) below the tab -
1 - index finger (forefinger)
2 - middle finger
3 - ring finger (annular)
4 - little finger (pinky)

===    - note held, length of note, duration
=-=    - let the note ring out (don't dampen the sound)
>      - (above the tab) accented, marked, stressed
|-3-|  - triplet - 3 notes played in the time of 2 notes
< < <  - crescendo - growing louder, becoming gradually louder
> > >  - decrescendo - growing softer, becoming gradually softer
arp.   - ripple upwards from the lowest note to the highest note
cresc. - crescendo - growing louder, becoming gradually louder
dolce  - sweetly
f      - forte - loud, strong
g      - grace note (ornament) - an extra note played before the main note -
         the next note is usually played as a hammer-on, pull-off or slide
h      - hammer-on
mf     - mezzoforte - moderately loud
p      - (below the tab) piano - soft, quietly
p      - (between the notes) pull-off
poco   - little, a little (poco cresc. - growing a little louder)
pp     - pianissimo - very soft
pizz.  - pizzicato - muted (usually played by plucking the string with the
         right thumb and using the palm of the hand to mute the sting)
tr.    - trill (ornament) - a rapid alternation of a note with the note above it
         in the scale (usually starting on the note above)
W      - upper mordent (ornament) - play main note, followed by the note above
         (in the scale), and then return to original note (it may be extended)
<7>    - natural (open) harmonic on the 7th fret
5<17>  - artificial harmonic, left hand 5th fret, right hand points to 17th fret

c1     - part barre on the 1st fret
C1     - barre on the 1st fret
c2     - part barre on the 2nd fret
C2     - barre on the 2nd fret [etc]
