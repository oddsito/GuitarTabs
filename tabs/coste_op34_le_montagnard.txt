/      - slide the note up
>      - accented, marked, stressed
|-3-|  - triplet - 3 notes played in the time of 2 notes
< < <  - crescendo - growing louder
> > >  - decrescendo - growing softer
arp.   - ripple upwards from the lowest note to the highest note

f      - forte - loud
ff     - fortissimo - very loud
F.     - Fermata - pause, note(s) held
fp     - fortepiano - loud then soft when repeating the section (?)
g      - grace note - ornament - an extra note played before the main
         note and immediately released - the following note is usually
         played as a hammer-on, a pull-off, or sometimes a slide
h      - hammer-on
mf     - mezzoforte - moderately loud
p      - (below the tab) piano - soft
p      - (between the notes) pull-off
rit.   - ritenuto - suddenly slowing down
<12>   - natural (open) (octave) harmonic on the 12th fret

c1  - part barre on the 1st fret
C1  - barre on the 1st fret
c2  - part barre on the 2nd fret
