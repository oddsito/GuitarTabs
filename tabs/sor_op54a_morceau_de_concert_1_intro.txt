Notes and Legend
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
The MIDI tempo of the Intro is 60 bpm and of the Theme 88 bpm (quarter notes).

measure 2 - the 1st 2 chords have a wedge above them, meaning either accented
            or stacatissimo (note held for only a quarter of its value)
measure 4 - in the sheet there is an extra quarter note rest after the 1st chord
measure 5 - in the sheet the 2nd lowest note of the 2 chords is C (not C#)
measure 12 - in the sheet the 1st note of the sixteenth notes triplet (C#) is
             shown as being played at the same time as the last note of the
             2nd set of eighth note triplets
measure 28 - in both reference videos most of the 32nd notes on the 3rd beat
             are played - hammers and pull-offs have been used in the tab to
             show the phrasing - the sheet isn't clear which strings should
             be used for the 32nd notes
measure 39 - in the sheet the 1st 2 notes are both quarter notes
measure 46 - in the sheet the bottom note of the 1st chord is a dotted quarter
             note (not a half note)

Left Hand Fingering (LHF) below the tab -
1 - index finger (forefinger)
2 - middle finger
3 - ring finger
4 - little finger (pinkie)

===    - note held, length of note, duration
|-3-|  - triplet - 3 notes played in the time of 2 notes
|-5-|  - pentuplet - 5 notes in the time of 4 notes
|-7-|  - septuplet - 7 notes in the time of 4 notes
cresc. - crescendo - becoming gradually louder
F.     - Fermata - pause, note held
f      - forte - loud, strong
g      - grace note - ornament - an extra note played on or just before the
         main note and immediately released - the following note is usually
         played as a hammer-on or a pull-off or sometimes a slide
h      - (between the notes) hammer-on
p      - (below the tab) piano - soft, quiet
p      - (between the notes) pull-off

c1     - part barre on the 1st fret
C1     - barre on the 1st fret
c2     - part barre on the 2nd fret
C2     - barre on the 2nd fret [etc]
